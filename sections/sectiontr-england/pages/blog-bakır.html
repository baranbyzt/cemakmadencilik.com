How We Extract Copper

Copper is a chemical element with the symbol Cu and atomic number 29. It is a soft, malleable and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. The newly exposed pure copper surface is pinkish-orange in color. Copper is used as a conductor of heat and electricity in building materials, in the composition of various metal alloys, in jewelry such as sterling silver, in the manufacture of cupronickel marine equipment and coins, and in constant load meters (English: strain gauge) and thermocouples measuring temperature.
