How We Extract Gold

Gold production is primarily carried out by extracting the metal from the ground, applying suitable processes for pre-enrichment, and then applying appropriate metallurgical (hydrometallurgy/pyrometallurgy) processes. In the early days of gold mining, production was carried out using simple miner pans or hydraulic mining. However, today, large-scale production companies carry out production in the form of open pit operation or mining in suitable locations. After the ore is mined, it can be enriched by heap leaching. At this stage, after the ore coming out of the ground is crushed/milled to the appropriate size, it is placed in a heap on a liquid impermeable layer. The cyanide used in the leaching stage dissolves the gold and binds to itself as ions. The solution is collected in special pools and used in further processes. Ores directly leached in this way are usually oxidized, low-grade ores. Because gold metal of high grade or sulfur content is resistant to cyanide, it must be oxidized by roasting or a similar process before cyanide leaching.
